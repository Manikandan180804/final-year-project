ABSTRACT
Rockfall incidents in open-pit mines pose significant risks to worker safety, heavy machinery, and operational stability. Current monitoring methods often react after incidents happen. They rely on manual inspections and standalone sensor systems that have limitations. To tackle these issues, this paper introduces Mine Guard AI, a hybrid predictive rockfall management system. This intelligent, real-time safety framework combines geotechnical sensors, computer vision, and weather data to predict hazards proactively. The system gathers data on displacement, pore pressure, strain, and tilt from IoT-enabled sensors. It integrates this with real-time rainfall and environmental information. A YOLOv8-based deep learning model identifies and pinpoints surface cracks from surveillance images. A hybrid AI fusion engine, using Random Forest and XGBoost, analyzes trends from sensors and visual irregularities. This generates a dynamic rockfall risk probability score and a real-time hazard heatmap visualization. To promote transparency and trust in decision-making, an Explainable AI module, powered by a local Large Language Model, offers clear reasoning for predictions. This allows for operator validation of the system's output. Designed for edge deployment on platforms like NVIDIA Jetson, the system can function offline in remote mining areas with limited connectivity. Mine Guard AI changes traditional slope monitoring into a proactive early-warning system. It sends instant alerts through dashboards and SMS notifications, helping to lower accident risks, reduce downtime, and protect critical infrastructure. By merging IoT, deep learning, predictive analytics, and explainable AI into a scalable and usable framework, this solution aids in smart management of geotechnical disasters. It also significantly improves safety standards in modern mining operations.
